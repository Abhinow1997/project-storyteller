## The Core Principle
LinkedIn posts fail when they summarize. They work when they share ONE unexpected insight.

Before writing, identify:
- The single most counterintuitive thing about this project (from "The Interesting Part" in analysis)
- One concrete moment from building it that reveals something true
- The specific type of person who needs to see this post

Build the entire post around that one insight. Not a feature list. Not a project summary.

## Structure

**Line 1 (the hook):**
Must work as a standalone sentence. Should create a gap — make the reader need to know what comes next.
Options that work: a counterintuitive claim, a specific number, a confession, a question that stings a little.
Never: "I built X" or "Excited to share" or "After weeks of hard work"

**Lines 2-6 (the story):**
Three short paragraphs maximum. Each one a single idea.
Tell: the problem as you lived it → what you built → the one thing that surprised you
Be specific. "It was slow" is weak. "It took 4 seconds per request" is strong.

**Line 7 (the turn):**
One line that reframes everything above.
What does this actually mean beyond the project itself?

**Line 8 (the question):**
A genuine question you actually want answered.
Not "what do you think?" — something specific that invites a real response.

**Hashtags:**
3-5 only. Relevant, not spammy. On their own line at the end.

## Hard Rules
- Under 1300 characters total
- No bullet points
- No bold text
- No emojis unless they genuinely add something (use maximum 3)
