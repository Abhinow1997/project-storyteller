Using the full project analysis from this session, write a narrative nonfiction article to ./output/article.md

## Voice & Style
The Atlantic / NYT Magazine. Literary, human, slightly urgent.
Write for a smart non-technical reader who needs to understand why this software matters.

## Structure (follow this arc)

### Opening Scene
Invent a realistic character — a specific person with a job title, a frustration, a moment.
Drop them into the exact problem this project solves. No thesis yet. Just the scene.
Make the reader feel the friction before you name the solution.

### The Gap
Transition from the character's problem to the broader reality.
Why does this problem exist? What does the landscape look like before this project?

### The Approach  
Introduce the project as a response, not a product.
Explain the architectural decisions as human choices — tradeoffs made by a person with beliefs.
Use the "Biggest Design Decision" from the analysis here.
Never say "the code does X" — say "the system chooses to X, which means Y for the user"

### The Interesting Part
Take the most surprising/counterintuitive finding from analysis and give it a full section.
This is where technical readers lean in and non-technical readers feel smart.

### What It Gets Right (and What It Doesn't)
Be honest. Use the gaps from the analysis.
Good narrative nonfiction acknowledges complexity.

### The Bigger Picture
Close with what this project represents.
Connect it to something larger — a trend, a philosophy, a shift in how we build things.
Last paragraph should land with weight.

## Image Prompts
Insert exactly 4 image prompts at natural visual transitions between sections.
Format exactly like this — on its own line:
